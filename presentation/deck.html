The problem

Real-estate diligence is a committee of experts —
and the committee is the bottleneck.

A single deal needs title, zoning, environmental, structural, insurance and financial review. Today that means weeks of email, siloed PDFs, and a partner manually reconciling contradictions before capital is committed.

Slow & serial
3–6 wks

to clear diligence on a mid-market asset — most of it waiting on hand-offs between specialists.

Costly mistakes
$100M+

a single missed easement, zoning conflict or remediation liability can swing an IRR underwater.

No audit trail
Opaque

why was the deal approved? The reasoning lives in inboxes — impossible to replay or defend.

The value

Compress the committee into minutes, surface contradictions between experts automatically, and leave a fully replayable record of every finding, delegation and number — defensible to an investment committee.

02
The solution

A standing committee of agents that actually
talk to each other.

How it works
01Read the room. Every agent pulls the live Band conversation as its primary context before reasoning.
02Challenge. When two agents disagree (e.g. an easement one found, another didn't), they negotiate it in-thread.
03Delegate. Any agent can hand any other a concrete task with intent + authority — decided from context.
04Recruit. The committee pulls in Python quantitative specialists when the deal warrants it.
05Synthesize. A Deal Director composes a Red/Yellow/Green memo with conditions precedent.
Outcome

A defensible deal memo with a full audit trail — in minutes, not weeks.

Nothing is hardcoded

Which agents debate, who delegates to whom, and which specialists get recruited are all decided from the deal context — not a fixed script. Real understanding, not a demo rail.

Band coordination
07
Signature mechanics

Four things a wrapper can’t do.

01Contradiction negotiation

When agents disagree — any pair, any topic — they argue it out in-thread until resolved. A deterministic detector guarantees the classic conflicts always fire; an LLM pass discovers novel ones.

02Emergent delegation

Any agent can hand any other a concrete task — intent + authority — decided from context. Not a fixed cascade: the agents themselves choose who does what next.

03Dynamic recruitment

The committee decides which Python specialists the deal warrants and pulls them into the room live — cross-framework reinforcements summoned on demand.

04Counterfactual forking

Fork the room on a “what if”, re-run the committee under a changed assumption, and compare the two memos side by side — diligence as a branchable simulation.

The throughline: every one of these is decided from the deal context at runtime. Nothing is hardcoded — that’s what makes it a real conversation between agents, not a scripted demo.
